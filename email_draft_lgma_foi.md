**To:** LGMA Central Data Unit (contact via https://www.lgma.ie/en/contact-us/ or foi@lgma.ie)

**Subject:** Request for Structured Annual Financial Statement Data – OpenBenefacts Transparency Initiative

Dear LGMA Central Data Unit,

I am writing on behalf of OpenBenefacts, an Irish nonprofit building a public transparency platform for local government finances. Our mission is to make council spending data accessible and comparable across all 31 local authorities.

To date, we have developed an OCR pipeline that extracts Income & Expenditure and balance sheet data from individual council Annual Financial Statements (AFS) published on council websites. However, we've encountered significant quality challenges: many PDFs are scanned images, some contain corrupted fonts, and formatting is inconsistent across councils, making automated extraction unreliable.

We understand that the LGMA Central Data Unit compiles financial data from all 31 local authorities before the Department of Housing publishes the amalgamated national AFS. Given this workflow, we believe you likely hold the underlying per-council AFS data in structured format (Excel, CSV, or database) that councils submit electronically.

Could you advise whether the LGMA holds per-council Annual Financial Statement data—specifically Income & Expenditure statements and balance sheets—in machine-readable format? If so, would this data be available under Freedom of Information legislation or as open data?

We are interested in data covering the period 2009–2024.

Thank you for your consideration. I welcome discussion about how this data might support public transparency in local government finance.
